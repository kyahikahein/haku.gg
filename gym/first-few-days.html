my first few days at gym

okay, so… i officially became a gym person. well, kind of. more like a “newbie who doesn’t know what they’re doing but pretends they do.”

day 1:

walked in. immediately regretted it. everything looked huge, heavy, and intimidating. machines with buttons, weird levers, and weights that could probably crush a small car. i tried picking up a dumbbell—shake city. arms wobbling, legs weak, ego bruised. left feeling like i had just survived some kind of reality show challenge. soreness? already there. pride? also there, somehow.

day 2:

thought i’d be a little better. spoiler: i wasn’t. but i did figure out a couple of machines, and i lifted a slightly heavier dumbbell without dropping it. small victory! my legs were sore in places i didn’t know existed. honestly, walking felt like a new extreme sport.

day 3:

by now, my body is plotting against me. everything hurts, but weirdly, i kind of like it. i’m starting to understand the rhythm, and i actually feel like i’m accomplishing something. pull-ups are still a nightmare. squats? my legs scream “why??” every time. but that 5–10 minutes extra on the treadmill? feels like a medal-winning performance.

moral of the story so far: the gym is awkward, confusing, and painful, but showing up every day—even if you look like a fool—is progress. and honestly, those little victories feel bigger than they look.

here’s to more sweat, soreness, and small wins. because apparently these kind of small daily wins are what we all live for.
